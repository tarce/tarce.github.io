The Role of Head Movements and Vestibular and Visual Cues in Sound Localization

Author: Hans Wallach
Date: October, 1940

  • Timeline
  • Paper

Experiment 1

Subjects Tested: 10

This paper uses the same setup as found in On Sound Localization. The first experiment is sued to determin the effect of the vestibular system on localization.

Results
...both proprioceptive stimulation resulting from active head movements and visual cues for the displacement of the head were excluded. Quite substantial achievements in sound localization were shown to occur when only vestibular stimulation was thus admitted.

Experiment 2

Subjects Tested: 15
Results
...visual stimulation, equivalent to that which actual displacement of the head would give, suffices to determine the direction of sound.
Design Decisions
In order to eliminate vestibular stimulation, it seems to be necessary to keep the observer physically at rest and bring about a psychological state of motion by means of induced ego-movement.

While the steup used will not be described here (reference the paper for details), it can be replicated in VR.
