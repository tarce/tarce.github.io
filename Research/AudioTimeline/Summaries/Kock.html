Binaural Localization and Masking

Author: W. E. Kock
Date: November, 1950

  • Timeline
  • Paper

Experiments

Subjects Tested: Unknown
Binaural experiments are described which indicate that the ability of the brain to localize a desired sound and to suppress undesired sounds coming from other directions can be traced in part to the different times of arrival of a sound at the two ears.
Results

Of particular note is that sounds on the cone of confusion cause maximum impairment to discerning.

Design Decisions

Placement of sounds on the cone of confusion is possible in VR.
