The Influence of Eye Movement and Position on Auditory Localization

Author: T. A. Ryan & Frances Schehr
Date: April, 1941

  • Timeline
  • Paper
Summary

This is a repeat of an experiment performed earlier by Goldstein and Rosenthal-Veit in Über akustische Lokalisation und deren Beeinflußbarkeit durch andere Sinnesreize about how a simple shifting of the eyes could produce movement of a sound-target, a phenomenon first noted by Pierce in Studies in auditory and visual space perception. Results are inconclusive.

Design Decisions

Note that headsets like the HTC VIVE can be extended with eye tracking capabilities.
