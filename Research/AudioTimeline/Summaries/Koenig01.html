Subjective Effects in Binaural Hearing

Author: W. Koenig
Date: January, 1950

  • Timeline
  • Paper

Experiments

Subjects Tested: Unknown
...qualitative report of some experiments with a completely binaural telephone system [versus a] ...alternate system ...in which two receivers were fed from a common pick-up. A switching system enabled the listener to alternate between these two conditions without changing receivers.
Results
A remarkable property of the binaural system is its ability to "squelch" reverberation and background noises, as compared to the system with a common pick-up.
With the binaural system, each party [speech] could be distinguished separately, and it was fairly easy to understand... it was possible to understand speech under conditions of extremely high room noise...
... a pair of similar microphones was tried, with their outputs merged and fed to two receivers. This system showed no squelching effect, no matter where the microphones were placed
Design Decisions

It was necessary in this experiment to switch between 1 and 2 channel audio, which is possible in plguins for aduio.
