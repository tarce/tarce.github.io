Post-Rotatory Localization of Sound

Author: Malcolm D. Arnoult
Date: April, 1950

  • Timeline
  • Paper

Experiment 1

Subjects Tested: 4
[Subjects] were trained to localize a tonal stimulus at 10°-intervals over a range from 30° L to 30° R. They were then given 10 revolutions in a Barany chair at a rate of 30 r.p.m. and after coming to rest were required to localize the sounding source placed at 10° L, 0°, or 10° R in the horizontal plane. Every S received 40 trials with the stimulus in each of the above positions. Both clockwise and counter-clockwise rotations were used.
Results
(1) One second after the cessation of rotation, S tended to displace the sound in the direction of the preceding rotation. Of the total number of reports, 76% showed this type of displacement, 15% showed no displacement, and 9% showed displacement in the counter-rotatory direction. (2) The magnitude of spatial displacement... was between 10° and 20°. The duration of the displacement ranged from 2 to 27 sec., with a mean value of 14 sec.
Design Decisions

The author's use of a rotary chair is difficult to replicate directly in VR, however could be overcome with a device such as the Hololens.
