The Localization of Actual Sources of Sound

Author: S. S. Stevens & E. B. Newman
Date: April, 1936

  • Timeline

Experiment 1

Subjects Tested: 1
...[confirms] the hypothesis that the localization of low tones is made on the basis of phase-differences at the two ears, and that the localization of high tones is made on the basis of intensitive differences. There is a band of intermediate frequencies near 3000 [Hz] in which neither phase nor intensity is very effective and in which localization is poorest.
Design Decisions

The apparatus used could be replicated in VR, however more recent experiments better control for variables whie coming to the same result. With only one subject, the ability to test many subjects under controlled conditions is greater in VR.
