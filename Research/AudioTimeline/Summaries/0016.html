Two‐dimensional sound localization by human listeners

Author: J. C. Makous & J. C. Middlebrooks
Date: May, 1990

  • Timeline
  • Paper

Experiment 1

Subjects Tested: 6
The goals of this study were: (1) to assess the utility of the head-pointing response for use in continuing studies of sound localization and (2) to measure the spatial dependence of sound localization performanc
The open-loop (brief stimulus) trials tested the ability of subjects to localize sounds presented from an experimentally varied location.
Variable Description Value(s)
Azimuth Horizontal angular distance from median plane. -170° to +170° @ 10° increments
Elevation Vertical angular distacne from horizontal plane. -45° to +55° @ 10° increments
Distance The distance from the central point in the head. 1.2m radius
Sound Type The sound type used. 150ms noise (20ms on/off ramp) w/ 500ms delay.
Sound Frequency The sound frequency used. 1800Hz to 16kHz
Head orientation was monitored by an electromagnetic device at 50-ms intervals throughout the localization task.
Results

...for many frontal locations, were less than 5°. For frontal stimuli, there was a general decrease in performance as the angle between the stimulus and the vertical midline increased. Nevertheless, the majority of horizontal and vertical errors were less than 10°, even for stimuli behind the subject. I

Design Decisions

An important consideration when testing in VR is the update rate of the HMD. Also, by making the sound length short (150 ms), the head is effectively stationary at onset.

Experiment 2

Subjects Tested: 3
The closed-loop trials were intended to estimate the contribution of motor errors to the variability in the localization task.

Closed-loop trials were a continuous sound source.

Design Decisions
...to circumvent the difficulty in orienting to the rear, we tested a modification of the head-pointing response in which the subject pointed to rear locations with the back of his/her head. Inasmuch as this modification produced no reduction in the sizes of errors, and it produced an increase in responsevariability, we abandoned this modification.

Continuous sound sources are readily replicated in VR. Note the attempt to modify pointing that failed.
