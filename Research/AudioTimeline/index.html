3D Audio Timeline | Study Design

A look at study design in 3D audio over the years.

This timeline highlights some of the important research conducted in 3D Audio, with a specific focus on psychoacoustic localization studies. The focus of this timeline is to develop an understanding of important study design decisions and their impact on findings.

1876

April

  • Lord Rayleigh publishes the first sound localization study: On Our Perception of the Direction of a Source of Sound

1894

Unknown

  • Munsterberg & Pierce publish the first work on the effect of rotation on The Localization of Sound

1924

November

  • Ruckmick presents a review of localization research in Recent Acoustic Research

1928

November

  • Trimble decribes the modern and current theory of localization in The Theory of Sound Localization: A Restatement

1936

April

  • Stevens & Newman confirm the factors that influence high and low frequency localizatoin in The Localization of Actual Sources of Sound

1939

April

  • Wallach presents results on head movement and its effect On Sound Localization

1940

October

  • Wallach presents further findings on The Role of Head Movements and Vestibular and Visual Cues in Sound Localization

1941

April

  • Ryan & Schehr find little evidence of The Influence of Eye Movement and Position on Auditory Localization

1949

May

  • Clark & Graybiel present findings on The Effect of Angular Acceleration on Sound Localization: The Audiogyral Illusion

1950

April

  • Arnoult presents findings on Post-Rotatory Localization of Sound

1955

September

  • Sandel et al. report on IPD and IID frequency ranges in Localization of Sound from Single and Paired Sources

December

  • Held presents findings on Shifts in Binaural Localization after Prolonged Exposures to Atypical Combinations of Stimuli

1956

September

  • Zwislocki and Feldman report on the Just Noticeable Differences in Dichotic Phase

1958

April

  • Mills introduces the minimum audible angle (MAA): On the Minimum Audible Angle

1960

January

  • Mills reports on the JND in intesity in Lateralization of High‐Frequency Tones

1969

January

  • Blauert reports that frequency alone can account for Sound Localization in the Median Plane

1975

February

  • Searle et al. report perceptible frequency changes by the pinna, according to elevation, in Binaural pinna disparity: another auditory localization cue

1984

October

  • Oldfield & Parker study localization in both azimuth and elevation in Acuity of Sound Localisation: A Topography of Auditory Space. I. Normal Hearing Conditions

1989

February

  • Wightman & Kistler study localization responses using HRTFs vs free-field in Headphone Simulation of Free-field Listening. II. Psychophysical Validation

July

  • Middlebrooks et al. studies Directional sensitivity of sound‐pressure levels in the human ear canal

1990

May

  • Makous & Middlebrooks study localization using a 'facial pointing' in Two‐dimensional sound localization by human listeners

1991

February

  • Middlebrooks & Green review the current state of localization research in Sound localization by human listeners

2008
