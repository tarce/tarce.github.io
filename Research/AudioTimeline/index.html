3D Audio Timeline | Study Design

A look at study design in 3D audio over the years.

This timeline highlights some of the important research conducted in 3D Audio, with a specific focus on psychoacoustic localization studies. The focus of this timeline is to develop an understanding of important study design decisions and their impact on findings.

1876

April

  • Lord Rayleigh publishes the first sound localization study: On Our Perception of the Direction of a Source of Sound

1894

Unknown

  • H. Munsterberg & A. H. Pierce publish the first work on the effect of rotation on audio localization: The Localization of Sound

1924

November

  • Christian A. Ruckmick includes a review of localization research in Recent Acoustic Research

1928

November

  • Otic C. Trimble decribes the modern and current theory of localization in The Theory of Sound Localization: A Restatement

1941

April

  • T. A. Ryan & Frances Schehr find little evidence of The Influence of Eye Movement and Position on Auditory Localization

1949

May

  • Brant Clark & Ashton Graybiel present findings on The Effect of Angular Acceleration on Sound Localization: The Audiogyral Illusion

1950

April

  • Malcolm D. Arnoult presents findings on Post-Rotatory Localization of Sound

1955

December

  • Richard Held presents findings on Shifts in Binaural Localization after Prolonged Exposures to Atypical Combinations of Stimuli

1958

April

  • A. W. Mills introduces the minimum audible angle (MAA): On the Minimum Audible Angle

2008

August

  • Neal Kin, Vladimir Oksman, and Charles Bry file an application for an encryption patent application.
